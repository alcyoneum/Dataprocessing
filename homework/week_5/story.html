An interactive map that allows you to compare well-being across countries in Europe

The story behind the data

The Better Life Index allows you to compare well-being across countries, measuring several well-being indicators. The life satisfaction score evaluates how people see their life as a whole. It is based on the Cantril Ladder that rates people's life satisfaction on a scale from 0 to 10. Life expectancy measures how long average people are expected to live based on age-specific death rates that are prevailing. What are possible qualities in a country that can influence either life satisfaction or life expectancy? This visualization shows three qualities: employment rate, educational attainment, and self-reported health.

The employment rate in a country is the percentage of the working-age (16-64 years) population. Is it possible that employed people are more satisfied with their life than those who are not employed? And how does employment rate relate to life expectancy? Is your life expectancy lower when you are unemployed, because it is harder to support yourself?

The educational attainment is the percentage of the adult population (25-64 years) holding at least an upper secondary degree. Are people more satisfied with their life when holding a degree? Is life expectancy lower when educational attainment is high, because studying is stressful?

The self-reported health is the percentage of the population aged 15 years and older who reported their health as good in general. Are people more satisfied with their life when they feel healthy? Is life expectancy high when self-reported health is high as well?

The visualization will show you some interesting and unexpected results!
