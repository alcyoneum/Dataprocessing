The story behind the data

The visualization is based on data from the better life index of 2016. It allows you to compare well-being across countries in Europe. Life satisfaction score or life expectancy are shown per country. In addition, three indicators for well-being are shown: employment rate, educational attainment, and self reported-health. Do some of these indicators have an influence on life satisfaction or life expectancy? Portugal for example has a low life satisfaction, and the indicators: educational atainment and self reported health are low relatively to other countries. In contrast with Norway, which has a high life satisfaction, high educational atainment, and self reported health. It seems that educational atainment and self reported health have an influence on life satisfaction, while employment rate does not seem to influence life satisfaction a lot.
