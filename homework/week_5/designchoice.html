Better Life Index

Minor Progammeren (UvA) - DataProcessing

Shan Shan Huang (10768793)

An interactive map that allows you to compare well-being across countries in Europe

The reason behind the design

Map of Europe

The Better Life index is an interesting dataset that allows you to compare well-being across countries. The reason I chose to draw a map is because a map is a very clear way to visualize variables per country. I decided to zoom in on Europe, because data was available for almost all countries in Europe, while data from countries in other continents was limited. I thought it interesting to see how life satisfaction and life expectancy is distributed in Europe, which is why I chose these two variables. The countries are coloured green based on their life satisfaction score and life expectancy. The darker the color the "better" the score or expectancy. I designed the buckets for the data to fill the countries, such that there are three classes: low, middle, high. The reason to do three buckets and not more was to keep it clear.

The bar chart

The bar chart contains three well-being indicators: employment rate, educational attainment, and self-reported health. I thought it was interesting to see how these three relate to each other. Does high educational attainment cause a high employment rate? Does high employment rate cause low self-reported health? A bar chart would make it easy to compare their relationship with eachother. I decided to do three indicators so that is was easy to keep the overview and because all three the indicators have the same scale on the y-axis ( % of population).

Interaction between map and bar chart

Personally, I think the interaction between the map and the bar chart is the most interesting one. I was curious to know which indicators might influence life satisfaction and life expectancy per country. So that is why I designed the map so that when a country is clicked, it will update the bar chart according to the country.

Overall design of the website

I tried to keep everything clear and simple. The index page with the visualization is divided in three parts: the buttons where you can update the map, the map of europe, and the bar chart. An interaction with one part, will lead to an update to the other. A navigation bar on top also divides the visualization from the data. As might be noticed, the general theme color is green. This color was chosen, because green is often associated with energy, growth, and harmony. This is perfect because it shows you data about life!
